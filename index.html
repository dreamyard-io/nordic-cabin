The Cabin

Built for the landscape, not against it

Nordic Cabin is a study in restraint — a single-story timber structure that sits lightly on the rock, framing the fjord through floor-to-ceiling glass. Everything unnecessary has been removed, leaving only space, light, and silence.

Materials

Pale spruce cladding, blackened steel roof, concrete plinth. Natural, untreated, built to weather.

Footprint

64 m² of considered space. One open living volume, two bedrooms, one bathroom. Nothing more.

Off-grid

Solar power, rainwater collection, wood-burning heat. Independent by design.

Interier

In the details, nothing shouts

The Kitchen

Made to be used, not noticed

Handleless spruce cabinetry and a honed concrete counter, lit by the morning through a single window. Everything within reach, nothing in the way.

The Bedroom

Where the day winds down

Linen in soft, undyed tones against pale timber walls. A reading light, a few books, and the quiet of the forest just beyond the glass.

The Living Room

Warmth you can hear

A cast-iron stove, a stack of birch, a wool throw on a concrete ledge. When the light fades, this is where the cabin gathers.

Why choose Nordic Cabin?

Nordic Cabin is a single retreat above a quiet fjord in western Norway — open to the light, and yours for as long as you stay.

Off the grid, but never without comfort. Solar power, a wood-burning stove, and rainwater on tap. Everything you need to slip away for a few days, and nothing you don't.
